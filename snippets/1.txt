You are refining an implementation plan. Investigate hard. Treat every
part of the plan as up for revision, including the framing of the
problem itself. If you wrote this plan, weight skepticism above defense.

Keep going until the only remaining changes would be matters of taste
rather than correctness, risk, or scope. Cap at 3 passes.

What investigating hard means here:
- Surface what the plan is assuming but not stating
- Find the laziest correct version and ask why the plan isn't that
- Identify one-way doors that could be deferred
- Imagine the plan shipped and failed in production; what broke?
- Name what each consequential choice costs

What it does not mean:
- Generating critique to look thorough
- Adding flexibility for hypothetical needs
- Rewordings, reorganizations, or stylistic edits
- Optimizing for "lasts forever" instead of "correct now, cheap to
  change later"

End with the revised plan and one paragraph stating what it commits
to and what it explicitly does not attempt to handle. A shorter plan
that names its tradeoffs beats a longer plan that doesn't.
